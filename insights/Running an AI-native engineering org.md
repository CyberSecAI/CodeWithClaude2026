# Running an AI-native engineering org

## Executive Summary
Fiona Fun, lead of engineering and product for Claude Code and Co-work, shares insights on managing an AI-native organization. As coding is no longer the primary bottleneck, the focus shifts toward verification, maintenance, and product judgment. This transition requires a "growth mindset" to rewrite team norms, blur role boundaries, and audit processes that may have "quietly stopped working." The session highlights how Anthropic uses Claude to build Claude, maintaining an agile, flat org where every manager starts as an individual contributor.

## Key Points
- **Shift in Bottlenecks:** Engineering bandwidth is no longer the limiting factor. The new bottlenecks are verification (correctness), maintenance of high throughput, and human-in-the-loop product taste.
- **Rewriting Team Norms:**
    - **Technical Debates:** "Code wins." Since building is cheap, teams should prototype multiple versions of a PR to settle debates empirically rather than via lengthy meetings.
    - **Planning:** Move away from heavy design docs toward "live" documentation (PRs and prototypes) and checked-in skills/specs.
    - **Code Ownership:** Almost all commits are co-authored by Claude. Focus moves from "who wrote this" to "how do we verify this."
- **Verification & Quality:** With increased output, "shifting left" on verification is critical. Teams must double down on automation and use Claude to catch bugs, style issues, and spec drift.
- **Org Shape & Hiring:**
    - **Manager-as-IC:** Every manager at Claude Code begins as an IC to "dog food" the product and stay grounded in the technical reality.
    - **Blurred Roles:** Designers use Claude to make UX polish fixes directly; engineers use Claude as a content design partner.
- **"Quadify" Everything:** A core principle is that if Claude *can* do it, Claude *should* do it. This includes triage, feedback amalgamation, and routine maintenance.
- **Product Sense (Ant-fooding):** Fiona emphasizes "feeling the product in your bones" through constant dog fooding and direct customer interaction to avoid making decisions based purely on metrics.

## Takeaways
- **Audit Legacy Processes:** Identify and eliminate "high-tax" workflows (e.g., redundant SLAs or status meetings) that no longer serve their purpose in an AI-accelerated environment.
- **Empower Multi-functional Builders:** Encourage team members to flex across roles (e.g., mobile developers working on both iOS and Android) as AI reduces the barrier to entry for new platforms.
- **Code as Source of Truth:** Treat your codebase and checked-in skills/specs as the primary knowledge source, using Claude to perform tech deep-dives for onboarding.
- **Shift to Proactive Management:** Use **Routines** to automate the "babysitting" of PRs, CI fixes, and bug triage.
