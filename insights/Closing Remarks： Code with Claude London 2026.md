# Closing Remarks: Code with Claude London 2026

## Executive Summary
The closing remarks of Code with Claude London 2026 summarize the shift from "synchronous coding" (manual PRs and reactive prompting) to "asynchronous coding" (autonomous agents handling the entire development lifecycle). The session emphasizes that we are at a unique moment in history where the "friction and slog" of software development is being replaced by intelligent agents. The final call to action is for developers to realize that the barrier between idea and execution is collapsing to zero, leaving only the question of "what will you build?"

## Key Points
- **The Falling Scaffolding:** Traditional restrictive "scaffolding" (manual boilerplate, repetitive testing, complex environment configuration) is being removed.
- **Asynchronous Engineering:** We are moving to a world where agents write, test, and deploy while the developer sleeps, enabled by features like **Routines** and **Scheduled Tasks**.
- **Collapsing Distance:** The gap between having a "brilliant idea" and seeing it "run in reality" is rapidly disappearing.
- **Agentic Legacy:** Software development is being transformed from an exercise in "friction" to an exercise in "intent."

## Notable Quotes
- "The restrictive scaffolding is falling away. The intense friction and slog of the last decade... is actively being replaced by intelligent agents that write, test, and deploy while we sleep."
- "The distance between having a brilliant idea and actually seeing it run in reality is rapidly collapsing to zero."
- "So the only real question left for you is this: what will you build?"

## Takeaways
- **Embrace Autonomy:** Stop looking for "autocomplete" and start building "Asynchronous Agentic Workflows."
- **Focus on High-Level Goals:** As the "slog" disappears, re-invest your time into high-level product strategy, user experience, and novel architecture.
- **Challenge your Scope:** With the barrier to execution lowered, reconsider the scale and complexity of projects you are willing to undertake.
- **Think in "Intent":** Practice articulating your "intent" clearly and rigorously, as this is the primary fuel for the next generation of software engineering.
